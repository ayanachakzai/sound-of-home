# Doch: you stitch the sound of home

The visitor is the embroiderer. The page opens as a handwritten field note
over cloth. The display typography is Beth Ellen, paragraphs use the warmer
and more legible Alegreya Sans, and Urdu uses Playpen Sans Arabic. Process
titles are drawn character by character as SVG ink strokes; the Urdu line and
body arrive only after the last stroke settles. The writing floats directly
over the textile, with no cards or panels, held legible by a soft, edgeless falloff
in the cloth beneath it.

The stable font-change design is the baseline: Beth Ellen headings remain
fully filled and use a soft textile shadow, while the Urdu line and Alegreya
Sans body reveal through scroll-driven position, opacity, and blur. There is no
SVG handwriting, stroke/fill transition, timed text delay, or writing audio.
The archive and ethics annotations are full-screen scroll interludes between
chapters, rather than labels attached to the canvas. The cloth darkens and
softens while five real dataset artists change one at a time; a later four-beat
interlude turns through the project's ethical commitments. Each scene owns its
entrance, hold, and exit, so neither can overlap the neighbouring chapter.

Six scroll-led scenes explain the gathering, method, limits, ethics, and
tracing of the work. The cloth drifts and turns with scroll and responds
slightly to the pointer, while the traced marks move on a shallower parallax
layer so the background feels physically present.

**Reveal**: once the chalk pattern is traced, the visitor reaches a deliberate
threshold: “Stitch to reveal the dataset.” Pulling the needle across the seam
brings the blurred cloth into focus and opens the interactive dataset.

**Pull the thread**: press and drag. Running stitches follow your hand, and
every stitch you lay sews songs into the cloth. The music decides *where*
(radial: distance from the Balochi sound), you decide *when* and *how fast*.

**Structure**: a textile mandala. The 213 Balochi songs are sewn first as the
central medallion, home. The world's 2,269 songs radiate outward, each genre
its own angular wedge, radius = how far its sound is from Balochistan's.
Sewing order is closest-first, so the cloth always grows from home, outward.

**Audio (subtle, later)**: when a Balochi song is fastened, a short preview
plays quietly. Open item: data.json has no preview URLs and needs a small
collection pass (Spotify preview URLs are deprecated; likely self-hosted 15s
clips for the Balochi 213 only).

**Ending**: stop anytime and the cloth keeps your unfinished edge. Or hand the
needle back ("let it finish itself"). The finished cloth stays explorable
(hover any stitch → song). Clicking a mirror zooms from its exact position into
the song and its six closest sounds while the wider cloth dims and softens.

v0 in this folder proves the drag-stitch mechanic and radial structure with
procedural marks. Material/art pass (real thread texture, cloth relief) comes
only after the mechanic feels right. Assets follow interaction, not the
other way around this time.
